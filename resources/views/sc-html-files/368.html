Blackrock Inc Profile

BlackRock, Inc. (BLK.xnys) is an American multinational investment management corporation based in New York City, the United States. BlackRock provides investment management services to institutional clients and retail investors through various investment vehicles, and it’s the world's largest asset manager, handling over $9.49T (USD) in assets under management as of July 2021. BlackRock, Inc. has 70 offices in 30 countries with approximately a 16,500 workforce. Blackrock Inc’s top holdings include Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Facebook, Google, Wells Fargo & Co, and it is also the single largest shareholder in Deutsche Bank. BlackRock, Inc. stock (BLK.xnys) is traded on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE) as a Standard and Poor’s 500 (S&P-500) and Standard and Poor’s 100 (S&P-100) component with a $138.9B (USD) market cap. Following the company’s announcement for environmentally sustainable investments in 2020, BlackRock disclosed plans to sell $500M (USD) in coal investments. In the same year, Blackrock, Inc. was approved by the China Securities Regulatory Commission to set up a mutual fund business in China. BlackRock is under criticism because it is the largest investor in weapon manufacturers through its Shares U.S. Aerospace and Defense ETF division. It is also accused of allegedly taking advantage of its close ties with the Federal Reserve System and climate change inaction, especially for Amazon deforestation.

Investors are trading the difference in the value of security between the opening and closing of the trade. You can either hold a long position (Buy) or a short position (Sell). CFDs are considered short-term investments or trades, as CFDs tend to be used within shorter timeframes.
