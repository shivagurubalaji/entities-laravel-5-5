History of the Czech koruna

The Czech koruna is the official legal money of the Czech Republic, and its currency abbreviation is CZK. The koruna is made up of 100 halé. Because the Czech Republic is a member of the European Union (EU), it is legally obligated to adopt the euro currency at some point in the future, though this does not appear to be imminent.

What Currency Can I use in the Czech Republic?

What Currency Can I use
in the Czech Republic?

Only the Czech crown (eská koruna - CZK) may be used in the Czech Republic. With the exception of duty-free stores, the Czech Republic does not take foreign currencies. Some supermarkets, hotels, and other businesses have begun accepting EURO payments, although they are few and far between.
