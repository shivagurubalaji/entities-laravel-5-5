Manchester United (MANU) Profile

Manchester United Plc (MANU.xnys) operates as a professional sports club headquartered in Greater Manchester, the United Kingdom. The British club manages Manchester United Football Club and all affiliated club activities, including the media network (MUTV), foundation, fan club, news and sports features. The Club markets and sells branded sports apparel and other clothing and products through Manchester United branded retail centers and e-commerce, including Man Utd Mobile App, and through the company's partners' wholesale distribution channels. The company operates the largest football stadium in Britain, Old Trafford, and it also invests in properties. Manchester United is the UK’s most successful football team, having won more cups than any other club. It is one of the most widely supported football teams with over 75 million fans globally. Manchester United Plc (MANU.xnys) is listed on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE), has a $3.26B (USD) market cap, and approximately $630M (USD) in 2021 revenue. Following Manchester United’s announcement in April 2021 of the founding of the European Super League, an unprecedented public outcry and governmental backlash forced the Club to withdraw its anti-UEFA decision immediately. Manchester United welcomed back Cristiano Ronaldo and Tom Heaton alongside the additions of Jadon Sancho and Raphael Varane to the men’s first team. Old Trafford returned to full capacity, and the team’s manager Ole Gunnar Solskjaer has extended his contract with Manchester United Plc. The Club launched a new principal shirt partnership with TeamViewer, renewed three sponsorship deals, including DHL International GmbH (DHL), signed new contracts with Ecolab and Renewable Energy Group. Manchester United Plc (MANU.xnys) commenced a new UEFA three-year cycle and format with increased broadcast and sponsorship rights to £3.08B (GBP), approximately €3.06B (EUR).
