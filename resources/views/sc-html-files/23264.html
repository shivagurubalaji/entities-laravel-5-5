If you’re like most people, your goal is to make some money with trading. Investing in الفوركس or stocks are both great options but you probably want to know which of the two is better. 

The reality is that there’s no definite better choice and your decision should be based on the trading type that’s more suited to your goals and trading style. In this guide, we’ll cover an overview of forex investment و stock trading and we’ll detail how they differ.  

Stock and العملات الأجنبية market overview

العملات الأجنبية trading involves simultaneously buying and selling two currencies whereas stock trading involves buying and selling shares of individual companies. The mechanisms underlying these two forms of trading are very different and are advantageous under different situations. 

Although الفوركس و stock trading are marked mostly by their many differences, they also share some similarities.

  • Both involve taking advantage of short-term price shifts to make a profit.
  • Both stock and الفوركس traders rely on technical analysis and fundamental analysis to identify probable price movements and inform trading behaviour. 
  • Both markets require knowledge of the markets and lots of practice and discipline to succeed.  

Let’s now take a more in-depth look at how the الفوركس market compares to the stock market. 

العملات الأجنبية Stocks: The top eight differences 

  • Volume

With a daily global trading volume exceeding $6 trillion, the الفوركس market is the largest financial market in the world. The stock market is immensely popular but it only has a daily global trading volume of $84 billion. Market size matters in trading because the greater the market size, the greater its liquidity. 

  • السيولة

Liquidity is important because it makes it easier to trade an instrument. For instance, due to the الفوركس market’s high liquidity, you can make profits by short selling in a falling market. On the other hand, stock trading generally works best in rising markets because the stock market has low liquidity which makes it hard to short sell in falling markets. 

Note: Large popular stocks can also be very liquid because they have many shares available. However, once you move away from blue chips stocks become significantly less liquid. 

  • الرافعة المالية

One of the advantages in favour of الفوركس trading is the superior leverage offered by الفوركس brokers. Common leverage levels include 50:1, 100:1 or 200:1 with some brokers going as high as 500:1. With a 500:1 leverage, a trader needs only have 0.2% of the value they want to trade in their account. 

This is in contrast to the 2:1 leverage which is common among stockbrokers. Some stockbrokers go up to 50:1 leverage but a trader will still need 2% value of the stocks they are trading in their account.

Note: The high leverage which can make الفوركس trading lucrative can also make it very risky – the bigger the gains you can get, the bigger the potential losses as well. 

  • Range of choices

Approximately 630,000 companies are now being traded publicly all over the world. With thousands of potential stocks to choose from, you will probably find it harder to track the stock market compared to the الفوركس market. 

Most international الفوركس brokers will offer you between 40 and 70 currencies, however, there are only 18 common currency pairs that represent the majority of the trading volume in the الفوركس market.

  • Market Accessibility

Stock markets are available during stock exchange hours and several major exchanges including the NYSE and NASDAQ have introduced some extended trading hours. The catch, however, is that extended trading sessions are generally characterised by low trading volumes and low liquidity. 

العملات الأجنبية market sessions are technically restricted to business hours like the stock market, however, there are multiple markets all over the world in different time zones and so traders can participate in the markets 24 hours a day. The market only stops over weekends and holidays, but with the moving time zones, even these days are squeezed tighter, almost making الفوركس a 24/7 market. 

One drawback of the الفوركس market operating 24 hours is that the market is still changing even when you are not actively trading and you may have to work at odd hours to realise certain trades.  

  • Price influencers

العملات الأجنبية prices are predominantly affected by global news, exhibiting sensitivity to emerging political and economic situations in other countries. You’ll need to keep up with macroeconomic issues like GDP, regional employment growth, and political prospects.  

Stock prices may respond to large-scale movements in macroeconomic conditions, but they normally respond to news or earnings reports about the company’s underlying the stock or the sector the company is in. Knowledge of metrics like a company’s cash flow, profitability, and debt is essential.

  • Cost

العملات الأجنبية brokers typically charge no commission, making their margin on the spread instead. العملات الأجنبية majors normally have extremely low spreads and transaction costs because high liquidity tends to equate to tighter spreads and lower transaction costs. 

Exotic currency pairs are far less liquid and so they have higher spread and higher transactions costs compared to major and minor pairs. Similarly, the stock market has lower liquidity, resulting in higher transaction costs. Stock traders have to pay both the spread and commission to a broker. 

  • التراخيص

Stock trading on all major exchanges has many regulations and limits but الفوركس trading is a lot less regulated which has both positive and negative effects. The major stock exchanges may impose some unwelcome limits but this protects you to a degree that trading with some الفوركس brokers doesn’t. However, this is where your due diligence pays off when choosing a regulated broker.

Forex investment أو stock trading: Which is better for you?

Some traders choose to trade both الفوركس and stocks but if you have to choose one, it all comes down to your knowledge of each market, your goals, your trading style, and risk appetite. Both markets offer diverse investment opportunities with different perks and risks, so you have to choose what works best for you.
